      Earth science module;
      study of Singapore's physical environment;
      Singapore used to be way bigger historically and naturally (without land reclamation) due to lower sea levels with more land above sea. Due to the melting of ice, a lot of land has been submerged and is under the sea.
    • Singapore's sea level would rise by 5m in 100 years.
    • Wilson cycle; millions of years ago the world was a big giant continent, which separated due to tectonic movements.
    • Marsupials; Australia continent associated animals (marsupials have like pouches) (koala, kangaroos), are also found in South America. This is because the continents were once connected.
    • Overlapping rock formation; the older rock is being cut, the one doing the cutting is younger.
    • Study of rocks tell us the conditions of when they were formed, but not now.
    • Igneous rocks of Singapore; oldest being chua chu kang granite. Other granite includes Dairy Farm granite, etc...
    • Rift valleys; formed by tectonic movements; They are hills separated by a body of water in the middle.
    • Geology is very important; like digging to build infrastructure in Singapore, you need to know what's beneath, what type of rocks, marine mud, etc.
